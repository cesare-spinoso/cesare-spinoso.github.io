Exercise. Prove that any DFA that accepts the language \(L = \{w \in \Sigma^* : w \text{ starts with 01 and ends with 10}\}\) must have at least 6 states.

To prove this, you might have simply created a DFA and applied the state reduction algorithm. But there is a more direct way of showing this! Firstly, we must build a DFA that accepts the language \(L\) and that has exactly 6 states. If you're struggling on this part, no worries! Check out the solution video in the DFA section. Once you have a DFA that accepts \(L\) and has exactly 6 states, you can prove, by contradiction, that there cannot be a DFA that accepts \(L\) and has fewer than 6 states. The proof technique that I give is applicable in general and is in fact how you would prove the correctness of the state reduction algorithm.
